      My brother and I took our parents here for their birthdays (same day!) earlier this year and simply put, it was one of the best meals I've ever had in my life. We made a reservation for a Sunday night and we were seated promptly after our arrival. We were probably the most dressed up group there; My dad and brother were wearing a dress shirt + jacket and I was wearing a casual dress. Maybe it's just me, but I felt like everyone else there was underdressed (t-shirts and shorts?)...

      My bone-in medium rare filet mignon was amazing. It was cooked to perfection and had the right amount of rareness that I love in a steak. The texture of the meat was perfect and nearly melted in my mouth. It wasn't overly seasoned and allowed the flavor of the meat to come out. The Mac and Cheese with truffle oil was crazy delicious.

      What we had:
      - Tuna Trio Appetizer: carpaccio, tartare, sashimi
      - Hamachi Shots (this was a big mouthful, but very flavorful!)
      - Raw Oysters (had a yuzu foam to put on top, I made the mistake of putting too much on at first, which made it very sour)
      - porterhouse (split between two) (this came with a very flavorful truffle salt, which enhanced the flavors)
      - bone-in filet mignon (split between two)
      - Mac and Cheese
      - Twice Baked Potatoes
      - Shrimp Salad
      - Pear Sorbet
      - "Kiss from a Rose" dessert

      I would definitely recommend everything that we had!

      Highlights:
      - free champagne + dessert for celebrating birthdays
      - great service, our waitress came at the right times and was not overly attentive
      - well-paced meal
      - palate cleansers (ours were hibiscus)
      - cotton candy at the end :)

      I would love to say that I'll be coming back as much as possible, but my wallet protests that thought :( Until next time Alexander's...

      PS: there were an astounding amount of little kids here, which kind of puzzled me since it is a rather pricey meal.

I was really surprised because it should of cost us around $14 since I only had one glass. We told the waiter that I think he charged us for a bottle and not a glass. He apologized to us and said he was going to fix it. My wine would of cost more than my entree. My bill went down to $158. We were very surprised! For the food and drinks we had, THAT'S A DEAL! My glass of wine was free, my boyfriend's extra glass of wine was free, our champagne was free, and our dessert of free. We loved our overall experience! We paid $200. The rest was tip. He also brought us cotton candy. It was the best cotton candy I ever had!

      I'm not a big fan of steak (or red meat in general), but Alexander's can have me slaughtering cows in a heartbeat. This restaurant has served the best steak I've ever had in my life.

      I love that this location is tucked away in the suburbs and I don't have to fight with mobs of aggressive city people for parking or pay for valet.

      In the entryway, there is an intimidating selection of the finest livestock - *ahem* - steaks. I always marvel at how the steaks are showcased in glass in a clean and classy way. They do an amazing job of really keeping the whole restaurant looking sleek, but what else would you expect from a Michelin star restaurant.

      Favorites: popcorn crab & seared scallops. Despite being a steakhouse, I dine here specifically for the appetizers. I've had everything on the "hot shares" menu (except for the lamb riblets) and everything is superb. If you're on a date and would like to try multiple dishes, order one steak and have them cut it in half and served on two plates, then order a bunch of different appetizers.

      I also like that I can stroll in casually, despite being at an upscale restaurant, and feel comfortable and at home. I can enjoy an outstanding meal without feeling obligated to sit through a 3 hour, five-course destination type of meal.

      I didn't get mind blown for a restaurant new on the Michelin list but it was A-OK! Came here on a whim since it was all on our to try lists; made a reservation on the same day Sunday, and we were able to get a spot at 8! It was actually pretty empty.

      The ambiance was nice, and our waiter Scott was really attentive to us. We celebrated a friend's promotion and they gave us free champagne, a dessert that said congratulations, and banana bonanza cotton candy!

      On to the food!

      Side dishes- the mushroom risotto was my favorite!! The truffle oil fries were great too, except they got cold way too fast. Eat 'em while they're hot!

      Appetizers- the hamachi shots are a must. It's their signature dish, and even if it comes with 6, they're willing to add a couple more to tailor it to the table's request (we wanted 2 a person, so we ordered 8!)

      The popcorn crab was good but not great. I read Tina N's review, and she said save your $22 for the crab in batter, dipped in mayo+sirarcha. I have to say, I have to agree with that.

      Salad- We got the Ohitashi salad, which wasn't worth getting. It was basically spinach drenched in the Japanese soba noodle sauce (which was really salty)

      Steaks- 3 of us got the porterhouse based on a recommendation from a friend, and my other friend got the 10 oz filet mignon. The porterhouse was salty, and wasn't that impressive. 28 oz though, so now I have lunch AND dinner for today. But, I tasted the filet mignon (medium rare) and it was soft and tender!! I'm getting that next time.

      Dessert- Crowd favorite is the o2, but it really isn't that good, so try something else on the menu. The waiter suggested fried carrot cake.

      Overall, the ambiance is great for celebrating, or a place for a nice quiet dinner. We definitely enjoyed the presentation of each dish. I think I enjoyed the side dishes and appetizers more than the actual steak though, but that opinion may change once I try the wagyu. I also would come back to try their omakase ($130/pp), which is new on their menu. The whole table has to do it though, so go with explorers and adventure eaters :)

      In 2011, they've started the year with a second outpost in San Francisco and with their original Cupertino location receiving a prestigious Michelin star. For a steakhouse, that's quite a feat, but Alexander's has always been about more than just serving steaks.

      For fans, it's a combination of stellar food, VIP service, and flawless execution. As the SF post is having some growing pains (see Michael Bauer review), it's clear that the perfect pace and ambiance of the Cupertino location is no accident.

      For critics, Alexander's is expensive and there's no arguing that some entrees (particularly the ultra exclusive Japanese Wagyu beef) are priced extraordinarily high. And while Alexander's would never be considered cheap, I'd argue that I could choose a menu of hits that would average about $50/person and have service that would make you feel as if you had spend several times that amount.

      For our most recent dining experience, we got a glimpse into the inner workings of Alexander's Steakhouse. Calm, serene, and organized, the kitchen is a well-oiled machine and everyone from the sous-chefs to the line cooks know their job and role well. While Gordon Ramsey's Hells Kitchen or Top Chef may thrive on chaos, lexander's is more focused on churning out gorgeous and delicious food. Some of our favorites:

      Hamachi Shots - their signature appetizer that introduced steak lovers to the wonders of raw fish. Typically ordered as a six pack of shots, they can also be ordered individually and are a bargain if you order one or two just to sample

      Lobster Mac and Cheese - although a small sized side entree, the truffle oil enhanced Mac and Cheese will get your stomach growling from afar. I think the large lobster chunks that can be added to the normal Mac and Cheese are a steal as well. All claw pieces of meat that burst from the Mac and Cheese for a slight extra cost.

      Prime Rib - I've had prime rib at about every well known restaurant in California and in Chicago. I think the prime rib at Alexander's stands head and shoulders above the rest. A huge slice at either 16 or 20 ounces, it truly is the steak bargain on the menu and has the most fans. While expense account tables go for the Wagyu, I almost order the prime rib. It's that good.

      Souffle - several restaurants along the Peninsula feature souffles but I really like the variety and ingredients here. They have a great pastry chef here and she continually reinvents this most popular dish. Whether it's chocolate, apple, vanilla creme, or banana, it's always good. In fact, I don't think I've tried any other dessert.

      Cotton Candy - it's a small thing, but the complimentary cotton candy is always a treat and it's fun if you're allowed to go in back and make your own. Although Alexander's is a grown-up steakhouse, I've yet to see anyone not smile when that cotton candy comes out.

      In addition to the food and desserts, Alexander's also has an award winning Sommelier and wine list (although the corkage fees for bringing your own wine are ridiculous) as well as some of the best servers I've had at any restaurant. I've come here casually dressed and with a tie, been here with twenty year old college students and with professionals, and the service has always been the same. The servers, cooks, and waiters work hard here and Alexander's is one of the few times I think that the tipping practice in the U.S. (compared to Asia for example) is very well deserved and then some.

      As you can tell, I love this steakhouse and our recent meal here only served to confirm that. One of our best meals ever and we even had leftovers to boot.

      Alexander's Steakhouse accepts all credit cards, including American Express. Special thanks to Tania for the great experience at our table!

I saw them cut a few slices off the monstrous sized steak, attempt to chew one slice, put their knife down and ask "Who told us the steak here was good. They were wrong. I'm never getting the New York Steak again! I'm not even packing it to go, if i don't eat it fresh, i sure as hell am not eating is as a left over."

We had all three of the desserts available, and I didn't want to eat another bite after trying just one taste of any of them. Ginger Panna Cotta drowned in a dark, raspberry/ginger(?) goop that gave my palette about 5 too many after tastes I wanted to forget.

But the issue here is, a true 1-star michelin restaurant should never have slip-ups or mediocre dishes at all on any menu, valentines day prixe fixe or regular day ala carte! Right?

      A final word!, a must-try for me is the prime rib and rib-eye from what I hear are melt-in-your-mouth amazing. However, for the sake in integrity, I would not recommend this place as a full 5-star or promote it as a michelin star restaurant. I think that is something earned and should be demonstrated on any day, in any dish on any menu.

    Specialties

    Alexander's Steakhouse is a fine dining interpretation of the classic American Steakhouse with hints of Japanese influence laced into the menu, featuring Certified Angus Beef, corn fed from the mid-west. We have our own dry-aging room and are proud to feature 28 day dry aged steak for unparalleled flavor. In addition to our American Beef, we also serve true Japanese Imported Kobe beef of the highest grade and from various pre-fixtures. We take pride in serving the finest and most luxurious products available and are equally proud of our seafood selections. The wine philosophy at Alexander's Steakhouse is to provide exciting and interesting wines while holding true to the idea of a classic American Steakhouse. Chef Stout's menu provides an opportunity to step outside the classic range of steakhouse fare and explore some truly magnificent possibilities in pairing wine with cusine.
